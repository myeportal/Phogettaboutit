# PHOITALIA Brand Identity Guide

> **Brand architecture guardrail:** Treat **PHOITALIA** as the primary guest-facing hospitality brand. Treat **phogettaboutit.com** as a domain and marketing asset. Treat **PHOGETTABOUTIT** only as a secondary creative expression, campaign property, or legacy reference where strategically useful and legally cleared.

## Purpose
This guide translates the PHOITALIA naming direction into a client-ready identity system that can brief designers, sign fabricators, menu teams, web teams, packaging vendors, and hospitality operators.

## Brand Idea in One Line
**Vietnamese lunch energy. Italia dinner warmth. One memorable house.**

## Creative Rationale
PHOITALIA works when it is framed as a hospitality identity, not a novelty mashup. The coherence comes from rhythm, not forced fusion:
- lunch is bright, aromatic, quick, restorative, and herb-led
- dinner is warm, convivial, layered, slower, and comfort-led
- both dayparts share generosity, familiarity, and neighborhood repeat appeal

The brand should feel like one well-run restaurant with two emotional tempos.

## Identity Objectives
The identity system should communicate:
1. **Immediate concept clarity**
2. **Premium-but-approachable hospitality**
3. **Day-to-night versatility**
4. **Strong signage readability**
5. **Memorability without gimmick overload**

## Logo Direction Concepts

### Concept 1 — The Signature House Wordmark
A custom wordmark with confident letter spacing, subtle European hospitality character, and a refined `PHO` treatment that gives the name distinction without turning it into a joke. Best for primary signage, menu covers, and the website hero.

**Best use:** storefront, website masthead, menu cover, investor-facing brand presentations.

### Concept 2 — The Daypart Descriptor Lockup
A clean wordmark paired with a permanent descriptor line: **Vietnamese Lunch • Italia Dinner**. This is the clearest operational logo direction for first-location launch and wayfinding clarity.

**Best use:** exterior signs, Google Business imagery, packaging, window vinyl, launch collateral.

### Concept 3 — The Lantern Crest
A restrained badge or seal using architectural curves, a lantern-like oval, or framed monogram treatment to suggest warmth, tradition, and neighborhood permanence. It should feel classic enough for uniforms and glassware, not clubby or old-fashioned.

**Best use:** coasters, staff aprons, cocktail menus, stamps, embossing, loyalty assets.

### Concept 4 — The Noon-to-Night Monogram
A compact icon built from initials or a stylized `P` that can shift visually from fresh daytime application to warmer evening application through color rather than shape changes. Minimal, memorable, and digital-friendly.

**Best use:** social avatar, app icon, favicon, takeout stickers, embroidery, small-format signage.

### Concept 5 — The Trattoria Modernist Wordmark
A more editorial direction with slightly higher contrast letterforms and a polished restaurant sensibility. This version leans upscale-casual and works well if interiors become more design-forward.

**Best use:** premium menu moments, reservation pages, campaign creative, merch.

### Usage guidance
- Keep lunch-facing materials lighter, fresher, and higher-contrast.
- Keep dinner-facing materials warmer, deeper, and more atmospheric.
- Do not split into two disconnected brands; the same palette family should span both dayparts.

## Typography Recommendations

### Primary display direction
Choose a serif or serif-influenced display face with hospitality character, strong storefront readability, and enough personality to feel ownable.

### Secondary system direction
Use a clean sans serif for menus, operational signage, web UI, wayfinding, packaging details, and investor documents.

### Selection criteria
- readable from the road and across a dining room
- elegant without stiffness
- friendly numerals for pricing and hours
- strong digital legibility on mobile
- works in both all-caps signage and sentence-case editorial copy

### Recommended pairing style
- **Display:** character-rich serif, contemporary classic, or softened Roman-inspired face
- **Body/UI:** grotesk or neo-grotesk sans
- **Accent:** optional condensed sans for menu subheads or campaign moments only

## Packaging, Signage, and Uniform Guidelines

### Packaging
Packaging should look organized, useful, and premium without overdesign.
- prioritize broth-safe formats and neatly labeled takeout
- use one-color or two-color branding for cost discipline
- create lunch/dinner sticker cues through color bands or descriptor lines
- keep microcopy short, warm, and non-gimmicky

### Storefront signage
- hero sign should privilege **PHOITALIA** readability first
- descriptor line should clarify the offer immediately
- avoid novelty illustrations that cheapen the concept
- dimensional letters, painted metal, or warm-lit fabrication are preferred over plastic-looking execution

### Interior signage
- reinforce calm clarity: host, pickup, patio, restrooms, ordering guidance
- use one memorable statement wall moment max
- keep wayfinding clean and operationally useful

### Uniforms
- lunch: lighter shirts or aprons, crisp and energetic
- dinner: deeper tones, slightly more elevated layering
- overall: neat, modern, welcoming, never costume-like
- embroidery or chest mark should use the monogram or restrained wordmark, not oversized slogans

## Brand Application Examples

### Website
Homepage hero: **Pho by Day. Pasta by Night.**  
Descriptor line visible near the masthead.  
Daypart CTAs separated clearly: **Lunch Menu**, **Reserve Dinner**, **Order Takeout**.

### Menu system
Use one master identity with distinct lunch and dinner sections. Lunch menus should feel brisk and scan-friendly; dinner menus should feel warmer, more spacious, and slightly more editorial.

### Takeout
Bag sticker or label: **PHOITALIA** with a subtle lunch or dinner identifier. Keep containers tidy, clearly marked, and photo-worthy from above without relying on clutter.

### Social
Use noon-to-night transitions, broth steam, pasta texture, herb freshness, candlelight, cocktails, and room mood. Voice should be lightly witty, sensory, and credible.

### Storefront
Primary sign: **PHOITALIA**  
Secondary clarity line: **Vietnamese Lunch • Italia Dinner**  
Optional supporting domain mention only where clearly subordinate: **phogettaboutit.com**.

## Voice and Tone Notes
Write like a polished neighborhood restaurant with a point of view.

**Should feel:** warm, clear, lightly clever, appetite-forward, confident.  
**Should avoid:** parody, fake accents, mob clichés, forced fusion explanations, try-hard humor.

## Brand Guardrails
Always protect:
- clarity over cleverness
- food credibility over wordplay
- warmth over sarcasm
- repeatability over novelty
- one coherent house identity across both dayparts

Never imply:
- trademark clearance
- that `PHOGETTABOUTIT` is the primary restaurant mark
- that the concept depends on gimmick fusion to work
